Did You Know?

Waste Affects More Than You Think

Waste management is one of the most serious challenges in today’s sustainable cities, especially in Myanmar in 2025–2026. With cities growing rapidly, populations increasing, and infrastructure remaining limited, it is becoming harder to collect, sort, and properly dispose of waste. In many places, waste is still dumped in open areas or burned, which causes pollution and harms both the environment and public health. This makes waste management a crucial issue that affects the cleanliness, safety, and livability of a city.

n Yangon, waste management remains a major challenge due to overflowing garbage, poor waste collection, and limited recycling systems. Plastic waste and improper disposal often clog drainage systems, causing flooding and health risks, especially during the rainy season. Therefore, improving waste management through smarter and more sustainable solutions is important for building a better urban future.
